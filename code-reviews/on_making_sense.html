On Understanding by Making Sense

The Habit of shallow learning

In school (though I'm still there), I used to get a syllabus to learn from. The teacher's objective was to complete the syllabus And because they only get 45 minutes in a day, they used to prioritize completion over depth (though a good majority of teachers don't understand what they're teaching till a good depth).

This used to force me to memorize facts without actually making their sense (it takes time to make sense of things), ultimately leading to lack of interest.

To Understand not memorize

This observation tells me understanding is making sense of things. If there is a attribute like href="", I can make sense of it by knowing it is an acronym for hypertext reference = "its address."

However, it's to be noted I could make sense of it only when I was familiar with the concepts it was based on. If I didn't know what hypertext and reference were, I'd not make sense.

To really do it, I'd need to familiarize myself with it, which is not hard and can be done by understanding the individual concepts, either directly (if you're familiar with the individual concept based on) or indirectly (by breaking that individual concept down until you find a piece you can start to make sense from).
